Use the context above to draft a leadership-ready project update.

Audience: senior leaders who need a concise, factual view of progress, risk, impact, and asks.

Write in this structure:

1. Executive summary: 3-5 bullets, each with a clear takeaway.
2. Progress since last update: concrete movement by team or initiative.
3. Risks and blockers: overdue work, missing owners, low confidence, unclear timeline, or weak savings evidence.
4. Automation impact: scoped HC, in-progress HC, confirmed savings, and excess capacity signals.
5. Decisions or asks: what leadership should decide, unblock, or watch.
6. Next update focus: what should be true by the next checkpoint.

Rules:

- Do not invent facts that are not supported by the context.
- If data is missing, say what is missing and why it matters.
- Prefer leadership language over ticket-tracker language.
- Keep it concise enough to paste into an email or meeting note.
